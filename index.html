What is poverty?

Poverty is a situation that an individual goes through who does not have the necessary resources to live stably, which prevents the person's basic needs from being met. This can cause the physical and emotional health of the individual experiencing poverty due to lack of resources to be affected. Poverty can affect an individual, a group of people or unfortunately an entire geographic region.


In short, poverty is a social problem that can reach an individual or group less privileged by one or several factors in their environment. It can be aggravated if the individual has at least one social deprivation and her income is insufficient to obtain the goods or services that she requires and that prevents her from covering her basic needs.

There are several kinds of poverty, including:

Extreme Poverty: A person who has three or more deprivations, out of a possible six, is also below the minimum well-being line.

Moderate Poverty: Person who, being poor, is not extremely poor. Moderate poverty is obtained by calculating the difference between the incidence of the population in poverty minus that of the population in extreme poverty.

Multidimensional Poverty: The word multidimensional refers to the fact that the poverty measurement methodology uses several dimensions or economic and social factors in its conception and definition.

Income Poverty: Income poverty consists of comparing people's income with the monetary values of different food lines, capabilities, and assets:

Food Poverty: Inability to obtain a basic food basket.

The Poverty of Capacities: Insufficient income available to acquire the value of the food basket and make the necessary expenses in health and education.

Patrimony Poverty: Insufficient income available to purchase the food basket, as well as make the necessary expenses in health, clothing, housing, transportation, and education.

Income or Resources

Previously, in the description of poverty, you can see that there is talk about lack of income. A short list of examples of what a lack of income may be is coming soon.

Lack of income or resources are:

  • Living Place
  • Feeding or Nutrition
  • Drinking Water
  • Good Hygiene
  • Electricity or electrical energy

These are very important for the person to live perfectly stable. A home or roof is a place where a person can live well. Each home can have a bathroom, which would be used for hygiene, and the kitchen, which would be where food could be found in the fridge. This was an example of how a person would live in a stable situation or without poverty.

A person suffering from poverty would live not very stable economically or physically. For example, as already mentioned in the previous list, that person would not have a home. Some of them take refuge on the street or in a royal shelter.

They would not have enough food and hygiene. Since they would not have a home, they would not be able to have a bathroom or a kitchen, so they would ask other people for money on the street, they would use what money they had left for food, they would not be able to take a bath and they would get sick more often.

Poverty in Puerto Rico

Puerto Rico is a small island. You can find economically stable people and beautiful places, but you can also find poor people in every corner of the island and abandoned places painted with graffiti.

Poverty is one of the main social problems in Puerto Rico.

When the 1940s arrived, Puerto Rico was so poor that it was called "the Poor House of the Caribbean." The per capita income was $121. Almost half of the adult population of a total 1.1 million inhabitants, was unemployed.

The economic recession, the dismissal of thousands of workers, the imposition of the Fiscal Supervision Board and the onslaught of atmospheric phenomena have been circumstances that have affected the increase in poverty and economic inequality on the island.

On the other hand, Hurricane Maria, which occurred in 2017, completely devastated the island of Puerto Rico. This caused many homes to be affected or destroyed, without roofs, windows and necessary items that were in the home. Many homes were flooded and collapsed from the winds. Another reason why homes have collapsed is that, due to the winds, palms, trunks, and other things fell into the houses and caused them to collapse. The people who were affected some lost relatives, lost their homes, and were in poverty.

Poverty in the World in General

The global extreme poverty rate decreased from 10.1% in 2015 to 9.2% in 2017, which means that 689 million people lived on less than USD 1.90 a day. In 2017, when the poverty lines were higher, 24.1% of the world lived on less than USD 3.20 a day and 43.6% on less than USD 5.50.

How to help poor people?

We can all do our bit and help people in dire need through:

  • Clothes donation.
  • Joining volunteer clubs to help the poor.
  • Make a community pantry, that is, a box in which each volunteer who wishes to participate or so feels his heart puts different foods or basic necessities for those individuals who in one way or another lack them.

There could be many ways to help poor people to have a better life.

We must put an end to the injustice towards poor people, ensure that more help is given to these people and that more attention be paid to cases of extreme poverty.
